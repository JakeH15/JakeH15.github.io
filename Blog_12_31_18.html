Track to the Future Recap, 12-31-18

Track to the Future is the last three day race program of the year at the Lexus Velodrome in downtown Detroit. For those that don't know, the Lexus Velodrome is a 165 meter, indoor (under a bubble), wooden track with fifty degree banking in the turns. It's construction was put together by Dale and it is specifically designed for madison races. Dale is the driving force behind the operation and does an incredible job promoting and making sure that the racing is safe, fun, and difficult. Thanks to a live broadcast, a bar in the infield, music, and fantastic announcing the racing has an electric atmosphere and draws a large crowd.

Unfortunately, on my first day on the track, while warming up, my rear wheel slide out on the banking, and I face planted before sliding down the track and coming to a rest on the apron. I was disoriented getting up and had a weird headache in the back of my head from the impact to the face, so I decided to sit the first night of racing out. The next day, I was feeling better, so I got on the track in the morning and decided to race that evening. You're supposed to wait forty-eight hours before returning to activity after a concussion, so I decided to be extremely conservative in the races and just follow wheels. Despite that, I managed to get third in the last of the night. I instigated a breakaway and then bridged a half lap gap from the back of the field to the break. I didn't have anything left for the sprint though.

The second night of racing was total amateur hour for me. In the first scratch race I was a little too confident in my fitness and ended up off the back after doing too much work. In the elimination I was the first one out, which was not too unexpected because that is traditionally my worst race. In the last scratch race of the night, I was sitting second wheel (perfect position) and winding up my sprint, but I lost track of the laps and sat up on the last lap thinking that had been the sprint. I thought it was the last lap because the people in front of me were sprinting like it was, but I forgot that on the short track the sprint often takes two whole laps. At Dick Lane the sprint is usually one lap or less. It was a rooky mistake that I won't make it again. Thankfully I didn't cause a crash because we had a little bit of space between myself and the rider behind me and I held my line after the 'finish'.

Overall I was very dissapointed with my racing. I was expecting my fitness to not be where it needed to be to be competitive because it is the base season for me and this week was a rest week. I've done very little intensity as of late and tons of mileage. However, I did not expect my tactical game to be so incredibly poor. I'm looking forward to coming back over the summer when I'm in full track racing mode.
