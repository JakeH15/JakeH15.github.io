Back to School, 1-21-18

I'm now two weeks into the semester, and I am really excited about my classes. I'm taking Origin of Complex Life, Physics II, Scientif Rhetoric, Research Proposal, and five credit hours of undergraduate research (sixteen credit hours total). Like last semester, I like the way Physics II is taught in the life science section. Dr. Darnton does an excellent job of crafting lab activities and test questions to be very relavant to the types of problems a biologist might come across.

Work on this website has slowed down considerably. Also, I have not been coding nearly as much as I would like to. Over the winter break I was doing some python practice problems, getting started in R and Perl, and working on the code in Processing for Dr. Yunker's VIP team. However, since going back to school I haven't been able to dedicate as much time as I would like to coding projects. I'm hoping once I get back into the swing of things with school that will change. Besides enjoying the process of creating something, I think coding is an important skill to keep sharp.
