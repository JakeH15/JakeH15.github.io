February 10th, 2019

Snowflake Yeast, Settling Speed Selection Mode

This past week I was able to acquire the culture tubes, from Dr. Ratcliff's lab, needed to test run an experiment we would like to carry out this semester. The plan is to look at how the diameter of the settling device affects the hydrodynamics of settling and the evolution of experimentally evolved 'snowflake yeast'. Settling speed selection is a very accessible way to select for larger size. However, once the fitness gains from increased size start to drop off, other modes of improving fitness surface, such as better hydrodynamics from a change in body plan or individual cell shape (Ratcliff et al. 2013). Our experiment will look at how the settling tube's diameter impacts the hydrodynamics of the settling and what body plans and cell shapes differing diameters select for.

  1. Ratcliff, Pentz, and Travisano (2013). Tempo and Mode of Multicellular Adaptation in Experimentally Evolved Saccharomyces Crevisiae. Evolution, 67-6: 1573-1581

February 2nd, 2019

Snowflake Yeast, Settling Speed Selection Mode

While waiting on materials, we've decided to run a one week trial experiment where we increase the viscocity of the settling medium. To achieve the increased viscocity, we are using a 1:1 mixture of glycerol and growth media. From early videos, it appears that increasing the viscocity decreases the amount of updraft during settling; yeast that fall to the bottom stay at the bottome instead of being pushed back up.
