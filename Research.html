Research Interests

My undergraduate research was focused on how cells respond and adapt to their environment and what role physics plays in this process. I worked with the snowflake yeast system (clonal groups of S. cerevisiae) in Dr. Peter Yunker's lab at the Georgia Institute of Technology. I had one main project which becamse my undergraduate thesis: Effect of Hydrodynamic Regime on Snowflake Yeast Evolution. Other questions and projects that I was interested in were: under which conditions are non-clonal clusters preferred over conal clusters (the classic "coming together" vs "sticking together") and modeling the growth period of snowflake yeast as a branching process.

Currently I am pursuing a PhD at Dartmouth College in the Nadell Lab. While I am still interested in how cells respond and apapt to their environment, my work currently focuses on understanding how behaviors specific to the biofilm mode of bacterial life influence the assembly of microbial communities.
